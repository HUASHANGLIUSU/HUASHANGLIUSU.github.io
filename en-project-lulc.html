Remote Sensing · ENVI · Spatial Post-processing

Campus LULC Classification & Accuracy Validation

A land-cover study of ECNU's Minhang campus using 10 m four-band imagery, ENVI maximum likelihood classification, conservative object-level Python post-processing, and field-photo validation.

PythonENVIRasterioNumPyscikit-imageGIS

Overview

The workflow combines ENVI maximum likelihood classification, object-level spatial rule post-processing in Python, and field-sample accuracy assessment. Seven detailed classes are retained for mapping, while Water, TreeCover, Grassland, and Impervious / Artificial Surface are used for a consistent comparison with SinoLC-1.

Data & Study Area

  • ECNU Minhang Campus
  • Four bands: B02, B03, B04, B08
  • 10 m spatial resolution
  • 139 × 163 output grid
  • WGS 84 / UTM Zone 51N

Classification

  1. Create seven training ROI classes in ENVI
  2. Run Maximum Likelihood Classification
  3. Compute NDVI, NDWI, and local texture
  4. Apply class-specific spatial constraints
  5. Export FinalV3 and assess accuracy

Python Post-processing

This stage is rule-driven spatial post-processing rather than a newly trained deep-learning model. Rasterio, NumPy, SciPy, and scikit-image support water connectivity, track-ring structure, vegetation cleanup, residential impervious refinement, and conservative speckle removal.

Field Validation

The main assessment uses 35 effective field samples: 3 Water, 13 TreeCover, 8 Grassland, and 11 Impervious. The same photo-derived coordinates are sampled against both SinoLC-1 and FinalV3.

Accuracy

SinoLC-1 records 31.43% OA and 0.0071 Kappa. FinalV3 records 51.43% OA and 0.3246 Kappa. FinalV3 performs better on this sample set, but the overall accuracy remains limited.

Main Confusions

  • TreeCover → Impervious: 6 samples
  • Impervious → Grassland: 4 samples
  • TreeCover → Grassland: 3 samples
  • Grassland → TreeCover: 2 samples

Conclusion & Limitations

Spatial priors reduce obvious speckle and implausible structures in pixel-level classification, but visual continuity does not guarantee higher accuracy. Mixed pixels at 10 m resolution, similar spectra among campus surfaces, vegetation boundaries, and the limited sample distribution remain major constraints. Higher-resolution imagery, better-balanced field samples, and semantic segmentation are natural next steps.
